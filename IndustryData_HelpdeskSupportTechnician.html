Assessment 2 - IT Work by Group 3

Industry Data Report

Helpdesk Support Technician

Natalie Jaensch | Student Number: s3871504

Ideal Job

My ideal role is that of a desktop services administrator, or helpdesk support. I feel that a technical support role would combine my 15+ years of customer service experience with my learned IT skills. The desktop services administrator role that I found on Seek.com.au has a high level of customer service, with a focus on process improvement and innovation.

According to Burning Glass Technologies Top IT Occupations List of 2018 (BGTOCCs), ‘Help Desk Officer’ ranks as the 5th most advertised positions with 9,416 out of 121,997. The transition to ‘work from home’ arrangements in the wake of the global response to COVID-19 pandemic has prompted a surge in growth for the IT industry. The Job Outlook website shows strong growth forecast for the ICT Support Technicians with a current employee size of 65,300 workers (joboutlook.gov.au 2020). The gender share is only 22% female and with gender diversity a huge focus for most industries, particularly the Australian government’s Advancing Women in STEM strategy, now is a good time to get into the industry.

IT-Specific Skills

The required technical skills for a helpdesk support technician are:

  • Programming languages - HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Python
  • Common operating systems – Windows, Apple macOS, Linux
  • Software proficiency
  • Technical writing skills
  • Project management
  • Data analysis

    SQL, JavaScript and Java are the top three highest ranked skills on the Burning Glass Skills in Greatest Demand list. Microsoft Windows is #4 and LINUX is #13. Project management sits at #5, with Python appearing at #22. Technical support and customer service also appear on the list at number #11 and #15 respectively.

    The three most in-demand skills that are not in the required skill set for a helpdesk support technician are SAP, building relationships and business management.

    General Skills

    The top soft skills for most IT professionals, but particularly helpdesk support technicians are communication, troubleshooting, problem-solving, flexibility, attentiveness, creativity and innovation, and collaboration.

    Communication is the #1 generic skill in greatest demand with 44,367 listings out of 121,997, followed by problem-solving at #2. Collaboration is listed at #5, troubleshooting is #6 with 11,471 listings requiring these skills, and creativity ranks at #9.

    The three highest skills that have not been identified in the required skill set are organisational skills, planning and leadership.

    Summary

    Having reviewed the data from Burning Glass, my opinion of my ideal job hasn’t changed. I feel that I possess a lot of the soft skills required to be successful in this role, and I have made progress in building the hard skills as well.

    The data has highlighted to me the need to have a solid foundation in programming languages and SQL. These skills seem to be prevalent in a number of IT professions so learning these skills now will give me an advantage should the opportunity for a helpdesk support technician role come up.
